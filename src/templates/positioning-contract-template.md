## Example output
### Frame of Reference
- Category / substitute category / job frame: enterprise cloud-governance platform with compliance and cost-control workflows
- Why this frame is credible: the product already manages asset metadata, policy exceptions, and review exports for operations teams

### Audience Fit
- Primary segment: compliance operations leads in mid-to-large cloud environments
- Behavioral/context notes: works under audit deadlines, reviews dense dashboards, needs defensible exports and ownership trails
- Psychographic notes: low tolerance for hype, values clarity, proof, and operational control over novelty
- Evidence confidence: medium-high, based on stakeholder interviews, workflow review, and category pattern matching

### Points of Parity
- Table-stakes expectations: role-based access, audit trail, clear severity logic, exportability, security/compliance posture, stable dashboard behavior

### Points of Difference
- Distinct value claims: triage-first exception workflows, evidence-oriented exports, and a design system that keeps dense review readable
- Reason-to-believe for each: workflow structure is built around exception ownership, export specs preserve hierarchy and document integrity, and system constraints explicitly prioritize readability and compliance over decorative UI

### Distinctiveness vs Differentiation
- What must be recognizably ours: restrained operator tone, disciplined hierarchy, and a visual identity that signals competence rather than commodity SaaS sameness
- What is actually different in value: the product reduces review friction by making ownership, severity, and action paths visible without requiring a second analysis tool

### Trust and Risk
- Category risk level: high enough that unsubstantiated claims and over-expressive branding create trust loss
- Required trust signals: security/compliance posture, evidence-backed claims, export integrity, visible data freshness, contactability
- Claims that need substantiation: “audit-ready,” “secure,” “AI-assisted,” and any time or efficiency improvement claims

### Brand Architecture
- Master brand / endorsed / sub-brand / separate brand: master brand with descriptive product module naming
- Why: shared trust and governance matter more than creating expressive sub-brands

### Brand vs Interface
- What can stay expressive: identity moments, campaign messaging, selective display typography, restrained accent usage
- What must stay conventional/usable: forms, dashboards, exports, critical admin actions, severity indicators

### Validation Gaps
- Unsupported assumptions: whether buyers perceive “AI-assisted” as meaningful or risky in this category
- Research needed: claim-language testing, trust-signal prioritization, and category-comparison interviews with actual buyers
