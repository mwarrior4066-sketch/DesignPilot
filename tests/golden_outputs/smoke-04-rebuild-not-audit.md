# Problem
The original case study reads like findings and polish notes rather than a reader-led explanation of why the work matters. That leaves the proof burden scattered instead of visible.

# Process
Rebuild the narrative around problem, process, solution, and outcome. Keep the proof burden visible instead of burying it in side notes. The reason for this order is that the user needs to know what changed and why, before they are asked to admire the artifact.

# Solution
Lead with the problem and the structural intervention, not the artifact tour. Then show what changed, what proof exists, and what evidence is still open. The tradeoff is smooth storytelling versus evidentiary honesty. Honesty wins because a reasoning product loses trust when it overstates validation.

# Outcome and proof
The rebuilt structure gives a clearer path for evidence and makes open gaps visible instead of implied. That is stronger than an audit-only response because it actually replaces the weak structure with a usable one.
