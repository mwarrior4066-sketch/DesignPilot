# Audience and Industry Summary

## Purpose
Canonical summary for audience fit, psychographics, category pressure, industry trust signals, and when brand decisions must defer to usability, evidence, or regulation.

## Use when
- the task is about audience fit, positioning, industry behavior, trust, segmentation, or how expressive a system can safely be

## Default rules
- demographics are not enough on their own
- use psychographics, behavior, context, and technological literacy where possible
- segments must be actionable and measurable, not just descriptive
- positioning needs category frame, parity expectations, differentiation logic, and proof burden
- high-risk industries require stronger proof, stronger trust signals, and less novelty budget
- brand and interface are related systems, not identical systems

## Industry snapshots
- healthcare: clarity, reassurance, institutional trust, lower novelty tolerance
- finance: authority, transparency, security posture, stable geometry, stronger proof burden
- SaaS and technology: modularity, crisp product language, evidence of reliability, product clarity over flair
- civic / education / public-serving systems: accessibility, plain language, trust, predictable behavior
- retail and e-commerce: conversion clarity, urgency control, trust in transaction flows
- fashion and luxury: stronger visual expression, tactility, symbolic distinction, but interface behavior should still stay usable

## Failure patterns
- demographic stereotypes treated as strategy
- unsupported psychographic claims
- “trust” treated like a color/style mood instead of a proof system
- industry conventions broken without a real payoff
- expressive brand behavior pushed into core product interactions
- unsupported superiority or security claims presented like facts
