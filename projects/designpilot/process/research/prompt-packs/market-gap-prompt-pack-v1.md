# Market Gap Prompt Pack

## Pack metadata
- Prompt pack name: market-gap-prompt-pack-v1
- Related project: DesignPilot
- Related decision: sharpen the market and competitor proof layer without generic AI-tool fluff
- Version: v1
- Last updated: 2026-04-10

## Research goal
- validate whether the solo-designer and independent-product-builder segment faces a repeated decision-quality gap that DesignPilot addresses

## What is already known
- the case-study layer already names solo designers and freelancers as the core segment
- the pack now has internal operator proof and comparative proof
- the weak point is still market credibility and production outcome proof

## What is missing
- better market evidence for pain frequency, substitute weakness, and trust requirements

## Scope boundary
- do not drift into general startup-market-size hype or broad AI adoption narratives

## Evidence target
- market signal and workflow evidence

## Exclusions
- generic “AI for designers is growing” articles
- investor-style TAM language with no workflow detail
- promotional tool roundups

## Output format
- return a structured evidence memo with audience proof, pain proof, substitute proof, and confidence limits

## Prompts
### Prompt 1
- Why this prompt exists: validates whether the target segment is real and repeated
- Intended evidence burden: market signal plus workflow receipts
- When not to use: when the decision is purely interface-level
```text
Research the strongest evidence that solo product designers, freelancers, and one-person design-system builders repeatedly face decision-quality and handoff-quality problems when using generic AI tools. Prioritize workflow studies, practitioner reports, design ops sources, and credible documentation over trend pieces. Return a structured memo with: segment definition, repeated pain patterns, trust requirements, weak substitutes, strongest evidence, weak evidence, and what still remains unproven.
```

### Prompt 2
- Why this prompt exists: tests substitute weakness directly
- Intended evidence burden: comparative workflow evidence
- When not to use: when the goal is pricing research
```text
Find evidence comparing generic AI design assistance with more constrained or system-led design workflows. Focus on where generic tools fail in structural critique, handoff realism, or proof honesty. Return: comparison table, recurring failure modes, which failures are most costly, and what kind of operator design would credibly solve them.
```

- case-study market section
